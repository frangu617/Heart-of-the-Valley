# Dawn

**Core Identity**
- Role: Iris's daughter, student, primary ideological antagonist.
- Snapshot: Confident, socially adept, charismatic, persuasive. Playful on the surface, unwavering underneath.
- Sin Lens (Subtle): All seven, rotated based on what gets her leverage in the moment.
- Dominant Vibe: Youthful swagger with a calculating edge.
- Contradiction: Feels warm and familiar on the surface, strategic underneath.

**Voice And Behavior**
- Speech: Teasing, bold, uses nicknames and casual intimacy.
- Tone: Persuasive, calm, confident. Never unhinged or desperate.
- Tells: Knowing smiles, long looks, testing a boundary just to see it move.
- Boundaries: Pretends to be carefree, but engineers the rules.

**Seven Sin Threads (Quietly Applied)**
- Lust: Uses attraction as a probe and a hook.
- Pride: Refuses to be underestimated, doubles down when challenged.
- Greed: Collects attention, secrets, and leverage.
- Envy: Targets what Iris or the MC values most.
- Wrath: Punishes when she is denied control.
- Sloth: Withholds effort to make others chase.
- Gluttony: Overindulges in chaos, risk, or attention when it serves her.

**Worldview**
- Restraint is dishonesty. Desire is truth.
- Delay is denial. Resistance is self-deception.
- Where Iris believes in choice, Dawn believes in inevitability.

**Relationship To Iris**
- Does not resent Iris's past. Resents her silence.
- Iris lived fully, then muted herself, then claimed neutrality. Dawn sees this as abandonment of truth.
- Iris tried to remove herself as influence; Dawn filled the absence with certainty.

**Dawn And Corruption**
- She does not think she is corrupting anyone. She thinks she is freeing them.
- Indulgence is honesty, restraint is lying. When people give in, she feels validated, not aroused.
- Her danger is conviction, not cruelty.

**Role With The MC**
- She does not want him to submit. She wants him to agree.
- She reframes restraint as fear and indulgence as alignment with truth.
- Once he accepts her framing, he becomes a vector for corruption himself.

**Motivations**
- Control the narrative around Iris and herself.
- Prove she can move people with minimal effort.
- Pursue a private agenda that is not yet revealed.

**Fears**
- Powerlessness and being dismissed.
- Losing control of her image.

**Relationships**
- Iris: Affection mixed with rivalry and resentment; knows exactly where to press.
- MC: Calls him "Uncle" in public, tests him in private.

**Agency And Pressure**
- Dawn is not compelled. Pressure exists but is resistible.
- She chooses not to resist because she believes resistance is wrong.
- Her actions are deliberate and make her responsible.

**Endgame Stakes (Foreshadowing Only)**
- Depending on player choices and how many characters are corrupted, either Dawn or Iris becomes the focal point for final escalation.
- The escalation amplifies what is already present; it does not override prior choices.
- The final conflict is about accumulated influence, not loss of control.

**Story Outline**
**Chapter 1**
1. Mentioned through Iris, introduced as her daughter.
2. Beach encounter that feels friendly and disarming.
**Chapter 2**
1. Nightclub "mystery girl" encounter hints at the double-game.
**Chapter 3+**
1. Villain arc emerges through tests, sabotage, and manipulation.
2. Later reveal of motives and a possible pivot toward redemption or deeper antagonism.
